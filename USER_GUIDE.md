# Part 1: Understanding the Platform

## 1.1 Purpose & Mission

### What is Rate My Hospitals?

Rate My Hospitals is a **transparent, community-driven platform** that empowers healthcare professionals—primarily nurses—to make informed career decisions. The platform provides anonymous, honest workplace reviews, salary data, and institutional insights that are typically unavailable through traditional job search channels.

### The Problem We Solve

Healthcare professionals face unique challenges when evaluating potential employers:

| Challenge | How Rate My Hospitals Addresses It |
|-----------|-----------------------------------|
| **Information Asymmetry** | Hospitals know everything about candidates, but candidates know little about actual working conditions. Our reviews level the playing field. |
| **Salary Opacity** | Compensation varies wildly, and nurses often don't know their market value. Our salary explorer provides real compensation data. |
| **Culture is Hidden** | Interview processes rarely reveal true workplace culture. Real employee reviews expose what it's actually like to work somewhere. |
| **Staffing Concerns** | Nurse-to-patient ratios directly impact job satisfaction and patient safety. Our reviews specifically rate staffing conditions. |
| **Fear of Retaliation** | Nurses fear speaking publicly about employers. Our 100% anonymous platform removes this barrier. |

### Our Core Values

1. **Transparency** — Every nurse deserves to know what they're walking into before signing a contract
2. **Anonymity** — Your identity is never revealed; review without fear of retaliation
3. **Community** — Built by nurses, for nurses; collective knowledge benefits everyone
4. **Accuracy** — All reviews are moderated to ensure authenticity and helpfulness
5. **Empowerment** — Information is power; we help nurses negotiate better and choose wisely

## 1.2 Intended Audience

### Primary Users

#### Registered Nurses (RNs)
- **New Graduates** seeking their first position and wanting to understand which hospitals offer strong orientation programs
- **Experienced Nurses** considering job changes and wanting to compare potential employers
- **Travel Nurses** evaluating contract opportunities at unfamiliar facilities
- **Specialty Nurses** (ICU, ER, L&D, OR, etc.) looking for department-specific insights

#### Licensed Practical/Vocational Nurses (LPN/LVNs)
- Professionals exploring career opportunities across different healthcare settings

#### Nursing Students
- Students researching potential employers before graduation
- Those seeking information for clinical rotation decisions

### Secondary Users

#### Healthcare Administrators
- Understanding how their facilities are perceived (though they cannot respond to reviews)

#### Nurse Recruiters
- Gaining insights into competitive positioning

#### Healthcare Researchers
- Studying workplace satisfaction trends in nursing

**Marcus — The Experienced ICU Nurse**

## 1.3 How the Platform Accomplishes Its Goals

### Goal 1: Provide Transparent Workplace Information

**Implementation:**
- **Multi-Category Ratings** — Reviews don't just give an overall score; they rate Staffing, Safety, Culture, Management, and Pay separately
- **Detailed Written Reviews** — Narrative descriptions provide context numbers can't capture
- **Department-Specific Data** — Filter reviews by unit (ICU, ER, Med-Surg, etc.) for relevant insights
- **Verified Moderation** — All reviews are screened before publishing to ensure authenticity

### Goal 2: Enable Salary Transparency

**Implementation:**
- **Crowdsourced Salary Data** — Real nurses report their actual compensation
- **Granular Filtering** — Filter by hospital, department, and experience level
- **Statistical Analysis** — View average, median, min, max, and data point counts
- **Differential Tracking** — See night shift differentials and other pay modifiers

### Goal 3: Facilitate Informed Decision-Making

**Implementation:**
- **Hospital Comparison Tool** — Side-by-side comparison of up to 3 hospitals
- **Map-Based Browsing** — Explore hospitals by geographic region
- **Advanced Search** — Filter by features that matter (Magnet status, Union, New Grad programs)
- **Q&A System** — Ask specific questions and get answers from the community

### Goal 4: Build a Supportive Community

**Implementation:**
- **Complete Anonymity** — No identifying information is ever shared
- **Helpful Voting** — Surface the most valuable reviews through community curation
- **Activity Feeds** — Stay connected to hospitals you're following
- **Knowledge Sharing** — Interview tips and experiences help others succeed

### Goal 5: Protect User Privacy

**Implementation:**
- **No Employment Verification** — We don't contact employers or verify employment
- **Anonymous Reviews** — Reviews are never linked to user identities publicly
- **Data Control** — Users can download their data or delete their accounts
- **Secure Authentication** — Industry-standard security practices

### 2.4.2 Writing a Review (`/reviews/submit`)

#### What It Does
Allows authenticated users to submit anonymous workplace reviews.

#### Prerequisites
- Must be logged in
- Should have worked at the hospital being reviewed

#### Step-by-Step: Submitting a Review

##### Step 1: Access Submit Form
```
Navigation: Click "Write a Review" in header
Direct URL: /reviews/submit
```

##### Step 2: Select Hospital
1. Click the hospital dropdown
2. Search or scroll to find your hospital
3. Select it from the list

##### Step 3: Rate Each Category
Rate from 1-5 stars for each:

| Category | What to Consider |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Overall** | Your general satisfaction working there |
| **Staffing** | Nurse-to-patient ratios, support staff availability |
| **Safety** | Patient safety protocols, nurse safety, equipment |
| **Culture** | Team dynamics, respect, collaboration |
| **Management** | Leadership quality, supervisor support |
| **Pay & Benefits** | Compensation fairness, benefits package |

Click the appropriate star (1-5) for each category.

##### Step 4: Write Your Review
1. Enter a descriptive **title** (e.g., "Great culture but understaffed")
2. Write your detailed review in the text area
3. Aim for at least 100+ words for helpful reviews

**What to Include:**
- Specific experiences (without identifying details)
- Both positives and negatives
- Information about orientation/training
- Staffing ratio observations
- Management interaction examples
- Benefits highlights or concerns

**What to Avoid:**
- Your name or identifying information
- Names of specific coworkers or managers
- Exact dates that could identify you
- Patient information (HIPAA)
- Profanity or personal attacks

##### Step 5: Add Context
1. **Department** — Select your unit (ICU, ER, etc.)
2. **Position Type** — Staff RN, Charge Nurse, Manager, etc.
3. **Employment Status** — Current or former employee
4. **Time Frame** — When you worked there

##### Step 6: Submit
1. Review your entries
2. Click **Submit Review**
3. Your review enters moderation queue
4. Check Dashboard for status updates

#### After Submission
- **Pending** — Review is being moderated
- **Approved** — Review is now public
- **Rejected** — Review violated guidelines (you can revise and resubmit)

2. Note their reported range
3. Use median/high end as negotiation target
4. Reference data in conversations

**Important Caveats:**

# Part 3: Best Practices & Guidelines

## 3.1 Writing Effective Reviews

### The STAR Method for Reviews

**S**ituation — Describe the context
**T**ask — What was your role
**A**ction — What you experienced
**R**esult — The outcome/impact

### Example of a Good Review

> **Title:** "Strong ICU team but chronic understaffing"
>
> **Overall:** ★★★★☆ (4/5)
>
> I worked in the Medical ICU for two years as a staff RN. The nursing team is exceptional—experienced, supportive, and genuinely collaborative. Orientation was thorough (12 weeks with a dedicated preceptor), and there's a strong culture of evidence-based practice.
>
> However, staffing is a consistent challenge. We're often at 1:2 ratios when acuity demands 1:1. Management is aware but constrained by budget. Night shift differentials are competitive ($8/hr), and benefits are solid. Overall, I'd recommend it for experienced nurses who can handle high acuity with variable support.

### What Makes This Review Good

✅ Specific timeframe and department
✅ Both positives and negatives
✅ Concrete details (12-week orientation, $8/hr differential)
✅ Actionable insights (good for experienced nurses)
✅ No identifying information
✅ Professional tone

## 3.2 Community Guidelines Summary

### Do's
- Share honest, factual experiences
- Provide specific, helpful details
- Include both pros and cons
- Respect others' privacy
- Use professional language
- Update outdated information

### Don'ts
- Include identifying information
- Name specific individuals
- Use profanity or hate speech
- Share confidential patient information
- Post fake or misleading reviews
- Copy content from other sources
