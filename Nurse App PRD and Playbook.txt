# 🩺 Nurse Employment Experience App – Product Requirements Document (PRD)

---

## 1. **Objectives**

* Provide a secure, anonymous platform for nurses to rate and review their workplace experiences.
* Empower nurses to share insights on staffing, safety, pay, culture, and management without fear of retaliation.
* Help healthcare organizations identify trends and improve workplace conditions through aggregated, de-identified feedback.
* Ensure compliance with healthcare data privacy regulations (HIPAA, FERPA if applicable, and general data protection best practices).

---

## 2. **Target Users & Personas**

1. **Bedside Nurses** – Primary reviewers who want to share experiences and browse employer ratings.
2. **Nurse Managers / HR Staff** – Users who can access anonymized, aggregate data for insights.
3. **Prospective Employees** – Nurses considering new positions who want to evaluate employers before applying.

---

## 3. **Key Features (MVP vs. Extended)**

### MVP Features

* **Anonymous Rating System**

  * Star ratings (1–5 scale) across categories: staffing, pay/benefits, culture, safety, management support.
  * Optional free-text feedback (filtered for PHI & defamatory content).
* **Employer Directory & Search**

  * Searchable by name, location, facility type.
  * Browse reviews and aggregated scores.
* **Authentication**

  * Supabase auth (email or OAuth).
  * Ensure anonymity for review submissions (store user ID but de-identify outputs).
* **Moderation & Compliance**

  * Use content moderation APIs to prevent harmful or non-compliant submissions.

### Extended Features (Future Roadmap)

* Trend dashboards (per hospital, per state, national averages).
* Benchmark comparisons (e.g., compare hospitals in Michigan vs. national).
* Peer comments and “upvote/downvote” on reviews.
* Nurse mentoring & community resources.
* Mobile wrapper (React Native or Expo) for App Store/Play Store.

---

## 4. **Compliance & Privacy**

* **HIPAA**: No Protected Health Information (PHI) allowed in reviews; system must filter out patient identifiers.
* **FERPA**: If nursing schools are included, ensure student data confidentiality.
* **GDPR / US Privacy**: Allow users to request data deletion.
* **Anonymity Assurance**:

  * Reviews are stored with user IDs but displayed de-identified.
  * Apply row-level security (RLS) in Supabase to prevent data leakage.
* **Moderation**: AI-assisted text moderation (Google Perspective API or OpenAI moderation) for slurs, threats, or personally identifying details.

---

## 5. **System Architecture**

* **Frontend**: Next.js + ShadCN components for clean, mobile-friendly UI.
* **Backend**: Supabase (auth, database, RLS, APIs).
* **Hosting**: Vercel (frontend + edge functions).
* **Database**: Supabase Postgres with schema for Users, Employers, Ratings, Comments, Reports.
* **Integrations**: Moderation API, Analytics Dashboard (Phase 2).

---

## 6. **Roadmap & Phasing**

**Phase 1 (MVP – 3 months)**

* Employer directory & search.
* Anonymous ratings (core categories).
* Authentication & anonymity rules.
* Moderation pipeline.
* Vercel deployment.

**Phase 2 (Enhancements – 3–6 months)**

* Aggregated dashboards (per employer, state).
* Benchmarking features.
* Review filters (date, category).

**Phase 3 (Expansion – 6–12 months)**

* Peer comment/upvote system.
* Mentoring/community features.
* Mobile app deployment.
* Advanced analytics (export reports, HR dashboards).

---

## 7. **KPIs (Key Performance Indicators)**

* **Adoption Metrics**

  * # of registered nurse users.
  * # of employers reviewed.
* **Engagement Metrics**

  * Avg. # of reviews per user.
  * % of employers with ≥ 5 reviews.
* **Quality Metrics**

  * % of reviews flagged for moderation.
  * Average moderation turnaround time.
* **Impact Metrics**

  * % of users who report the platform influenced their career decisions.
  * # of employers who take documented action based on feedback.

# 🩺 Nurse Employment Experience App – Claude Playbook

---

## 1. **Design (Conceptualize & Frame the Idea)**

*Goal: Turn the concept of “nurse workplace rating” into a clear product vision.*

**Steps:**

1. **Define purpose** – Give nurses a safe, structured way to share honest feedback on their workplaces, while helping healthcare organizations learn and improve.
2. **User personas & stories** –

   * *As a bedside nurse, I want to rate my hospital on staffing, management, and safety so others understand working conditions.*
   * *As a nurse manager, I want aggregate reports to see where staff are struggling, so I can address concerns.*
3. **Feature brainstorming** –

   * Core: anonymous rating system, rating categories (staffing ratios, scheduling, safety, pay, culture), free-text feedback, search/filter for facilities.
   * Extras: trend reports, benchmarking by state/hospital type, peer comments, mentoring resources.
4. **User flows & wireframes** – Examples:

   * *Submit review flow* → login → select employer → rate categories → submit anonymously.
   * *View reviews flow* → browse facilities → filter by location/size → see ratings & comments.
5. **Constraints** –

   * **Privacy & compliance**: protect nurse identities, respect HIPAA by not exposing patient data.
   * **Moderation**: prevent defamatory or identifiable information.

**Deliverables:**

* Concept brief (why this matters to nurses)
* MVP feature list (ratings, search, anonymity)
* User flows (review submission, review browsing)
* Risk notes (legal/privacy, moderation, healthcare HR pushback)

---

## 2. **Plan (Architecture & Specifications)**

*Goal: Translate the idea into a technical & operational roadmap.*

**Steps:**

1. **Choose stack** –

   * Frontend: Next.js + ShadCN for clean UI.
   * Backend: Supabase (auth, database, RLS for tenanting data).
   * Hosting: Vercel.
   * Extras: moderation APIs (Perspective API), analytics dashboards.
2. **Define system architecture** –

   * Nurse user → Auth (Supabase) → Review DB → Aggregation & analytics → Display to other users.
3. **Design data model** –

   * Tables: *Users*, *Employers*, *Ratings*, *Comments*, *Reports*.
   * Relationships: one nurse can leave many ratings; one employer has many ratings.
4. **API contract** –

   * `POST /ratings` → submit anonymous review.
   * `GET /ratings?employer_id=123` → fetch reviews for hospital.
   * `GET /employers/search?q=“Detroit”` → search employers.
5. **Component breakdown** –

   * Review form, star rating widget, anonymous ID system, employer directory, analytics dashboard.
6. **Roadmap & phasing** –

   * MVP: rating submission + employer search.
   * Phase 2: aggregated reports + moderation tools.
   * Phase 3: mobile app wrapper, benchmarking dashboards.
7. **Testing & QA plan** –

   * Validate anonymity (test that user ID is never tied to review display).
   * Compliance checks (prevent PHI in submissions).

**Deliverables:**

* Technical architecture diagram
* Database schema with sample entries
* API endpoints list
* Component map (frontend)
* Phased roadmap (MVP → Phase 2 → Phase 3)

---

## 3. **Build (Implementation & Iteration)**

*Goal: Produce a working MVP and refine based on nurse feedback.*

**Steps:**

1. **Scaffold project** – Set up Next.js repo, Supabase DB, deploy on Vercel.
2. **Implement MVP features** –

   * Anonymous review submission (Supabase row-level security rules).
   * Employer search & browse.
   * Ratings display (averages + latest reviews).
3. **Integrate services** –

   * Moderation filters for toxic/defamatory content.
   * Secure authentication (nurses can log in but remain anonymous when posting).
4. **Test continuously** –

   * Unit tests for rating calculations.
   * Privacy tests (confirm reviews are de-identified).
5. **Document as you go** – README, setup docs, compliance guide.
6. **Deploy & monitor** – Ship MVP to Vercel, monitor usage.
7. **Iterate** – Add trend dashboards, nurse mentoring forums, “compare employers” tool.

**Deliverables:**

* Running MVP app (deployed on Vercel)
* README with setup instructions
* Privacy & compliance documentation
* Feedback loop for early nurse testers
